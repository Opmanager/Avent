Meet Our Consultants

The keys to success and productivity are already in our pocket. You just need someone to show you the lock and encourage you to open the door.

Dr. Reginald Stroble | Consultant

Dr. Reginald Stroble is a diversity and inclusion practitioner who strongly advocates for social justice & marginalized ... populations through advocacy, program implementation, workshop facilitation, and advising. Reggie specializes in creating student success initiatives for African American males & females while building strategic partnerships to enhance the appreciation of diversity and support initiatives for all underserved/ underrepresented groups.In his previous role, he served as Associate Director of Multicultural Affairs at James Madison University (JMU). He was responsible for graduate student and professional staff recruitment, hiring, and onboarding procedures. Also, he was responsible for developing designing, maintaining, and providing student leadership development opportunities for 30 or more student organizations.Prior to his role at JMU he served as Assistant Director for Multicultural Student Affairs at Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU).

Yolonda Edmond | Operations Manager

Yolonda Edmond obtained her Bachelor of Business Administration degree from Averett University. She has nearly a decade of experience ... working in the mental health field as a Mental Health Support Specialist and as a Quality Assurance specialist for non-profit and private agencies. She has aided and supported a diverse population of individuals with mental health and intellectual disabilities. Yolonda has provided social-inclusion for the individuals and their families with planning, developing, and implementing goal-oriented therapeutic services. She has supplied case management, conflict- resolution, and crisis management for those she serviced. Yolonda enjoys assisting others reach their aspirations in all aspects of life.

Robin Reese |
Consultant

Robin Reese (she/her/they) is a playwright and educator based in Pennsylvania. Her work explores the relationships between identity, power, resistance ... and inclusion. As an associate professor at Pennsylvania State University/Altoona College, she focuses on multicultural theatre, African American theatre history, Hip Hop Theatre, and acting, including Black Acting Methods. She has won awards for her plays and been produced in New York City and in regional theatres. In addition to theatre arts, she has been educating in social justice and anti-racism work, moderates white affinity groups and has a background in victim-witness advocacy and fundraising and board development for a variety of non-profits, including victim services. Her work also includes rape trauma and interracial adoption. Robin completed her M.F.A. in 1999 at The New School University/Actors Studio Drama School with a focus on acting, directing and education.

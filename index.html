Meet Our Consultants

The keys to success and productivity are already in our pocket. You just need someone to show you the lock and encourage you to open the door.

Dr. Reginald Stroble | Consultant

Dr. Reginald Stroble is a diversity and inclusion practitioner who strongly advocates for social justice & marginalized ... populations through advocacy, program implementation, workshop facilitation, and advising. Reggie specializes in creating student success initiatives for African American males & females while building strategic partnerships to enhance the appreciation of diversity and support initiatives for all underserved/ underrepresented groups.In his previous role, he served as Associate Director of Multicultural Affairs at James Madison University (JMU). He was responsible for graduate student and professional staff recruitment, hiring, and onboarding procedures. Also, he was responsible for developing designing, maintaining, and providing student leadership development opportunities for 30 or more student organizations.Prior to his role at JMU he served as Assistant Director for Multicultural Student Affairs at Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU).

Yolonda Edmond | Operations Manager

Yolonda Edmond obtained her Bachelor of Business Administration degree from Averett University. She has nearly a decade of experience ... working in the mental health field as a Mental Health Support Specialist and as a Quality Assurance specialist for non-profit and private agencies. Robin Reese |
Consultant

Robin Reese (she/her/they) is a playwright and educator based in Pennsylvania. Her work explores the relationships between identity, power, resistance ... and inclusion. As an associate professor at Pennsylvania State University/Altoona College, she focuses on multicultural theatre, African American theatre history, Hip Hop Theatre, and acting, including Black Acting Methods. She has won awards for her plays and been produced in New York City and in regional theatres. In addition to theatre arts, she has been educating in social justice and anti-racism work, moderates white affinity groups and has a background in victim-witness advocacy and fundraising and board development for a variety of non-profits, including victim services. Her work also includes rape trauma and interracial adoption. Robin completed her M.F.A. in 1999 at The New School University/Actors Studio Drama School with a focus on acting, directing and education.

Founder & CEO

Dr. Yolanda Avent

Dr. Yolanda L. Avent has been working in the field of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion for over 20 years and brings a wealth of experience working with organizations. She received her Bachelor’s degree in History from Virginia Tech, a Master’s degree in Criminal Justice from Armstrong Atlantic State University in Savannah, GA, and completed her Ph.D. in Curriculum and Instruction from Virginia Tech in May 2021.

Her content areas of expertise include but are not limited to Racial Equity, Implicit Bias, Identity Development, Microaggressions, Conflict Resolution, and Emotional Intelligence. She has worked in the areas of higher education, staff development, leadership development, and diversity for over 20 years. She has also served as an adjunct professor at Penn State University, Georgia Southern University, and Virginia Tech focusing on courses in Education, Diversity, and Leadership.

Avent served as a keynote speaker at the UnMasking Charlottesville Symposium held following the deadly riots, in which she addressed dismantling systemic racism. In 2007, she was awarded an opportunity through a grant with the National Science Foundation (Departmental Level Reform project, called “Reforming General Engineering and Biological Systems Engineering at Virginia Tech) to travel to Taiwan to conduct research in the area of student motivation & spiral curriculum in engineering and teach at National Cheng Kung University. Avent has been recognized locally, regionally, and nationally for presentations that focus on diversity and leadership, inclusive learning workplace diversity, microaggressions, diversity, cultural competence, black women’s identity, and contextual identity. Avent has provided diversity training for numerous corporations, professional organizations, and nonprofits. She is passionate about helping people reach their full potential as leaders and developing more equitable, diverse, international, knowledgeable leaders, and organizations.
